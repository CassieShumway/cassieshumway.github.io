Thinking With Type

By Ellen Lupton

Dumb Quotes

When quotations are used incorrectly, they are called dumb quotes or hatch/prime marks. Do you notice how these quotation marks are straight up and down? That's ugly and incorrect! The only time hatch marks should be used is when you're indicating inches and feet. For example: I am 5'3"

Simply put, don't ever use these as quotations.

"These are dumb quotes"

Smart Quotes

This is when quotations are used correctly. They are also commonly called curly quotes or typograher’s quotes. Notice how these quotations are distinctly opened and closed:

“These are smart quotes”

Em Dash

An em dash is one em wide. It tends to be used when you want to replace commas, colons/semicolons and periods to indicate an interruption, change of thought, or added empahsis.

En Dash

An en dash is half the width of an em. It's typically used for periods of time, connecting numbers and open compounds.

Here are the keystrokes for both em and en dashes:

Em Dash

—

option + shift + -

En Dash

–

option + -

X-Height

The height of lowercase letters are based on the height of the lowercase x.

The x-height is extremely important to the overall visual design. If the x-height is too small, it can dramatically affect the body text of an article, making it difficult to read. Its best to choose typefaces with a large x-height to maintain legibility.

Baseline

Where all the letters sit. This is used as a point of reference when aligning text, images, and other bodies of text.

Scale

Scale is important to create contrast in designs. By playing with different scales, you can give design more dynamic and depth. This helps to contribute to hierarchy

Font Smoothing

Many designers are utilizing font smoothing on their websites for clearer and crisper fonts. When using this hack, be sure to apply it to your whole stylesheet by placing it at the top of your code.

The antialiased disables subpixel-rendering and smoothes the font on the level of the pixel instead of the subpixel (source: Dmitry Fadeyev).
